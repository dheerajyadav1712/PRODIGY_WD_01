Beginner Training Plan - Detailed

This 6-week plan is designed for individuals new to running. The goal is to build endurance and improve running stamina gradually. Each week includes a mix of walking, jogging, and rest days for recovery.

Weekly Breakdown:

  • Week 1:

    Focus: Getting started with short intervals of walking and jogging.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 5 min warm-up walk + 1 min jog + 2 min walk (Repeat 6 times)
    • Day 2: Rest or light stretching
    • Day 3: 5 min warm-up walk + 1 min jog + 2 min walk (Repeat 8 times)
    • Day 4: Rest
    • Day 5: 10 min brisk walk
    • Day 6: 5 min warm-up walk + 1 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 6 times)
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 2:

    Focus: Building consistency and slightly increasing jogging intervals.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 5 min warm-up walk + 2 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 5 times)
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: 10 min brisk walk + 2 min jog + 2 min walk (Repeat 4 times)
    • Day 4: Rest or light yoga
    • Day 5: 5 min warm-up walk + 3 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 4 times)
    • Day 6: Easy 15-minute jog
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 3:

    Focus: Building endurance and reducing walk intervals.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 5 min warm-up walk + 3 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 5 times)
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: 20 min slow jog
    • Day 4: Rest
    • Day 5: 10 min warm-up + 4 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 3 times)
    • Day 6: 10 min brisk walk + 20 min jog
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 4:

    Focus: Transitioning to continuous jogging for longer periods.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 5 min warm-up walk + 5 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 4 times)
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: 25 min continuous jog
    • Day 4: Rest
    • Day 5: 5 min warm-up walk + 10 min jog + 1 min walk (Repeat 2 times)
    • Day 6: 30 min slow jog
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 5:

    Focus: Increasing endurance to continuous 30-minute jogging.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 10 min warm-up + 20 min jog
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: 30 min jog
    • Day 4: Rest
    • Day 5: 10 min brisk walk + 20 min jog + 5 min walk
    • Day 6: 35 min jog
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 6:

    Focus: Achieving a milestone of a continuous 5k jog.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 10 min warm-up walk + 30 min jog
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: 5k jog or 40 minutes continuous jog
    • Day 4: Rest
    • Day 5: 5 min warm-up + 25 min jog + 5 min brisk walk
    • Day 6: 5k jog (Celebrate your progress!)
    • Day 7: Rest

Tips for Beginners:

  • Always warm up before running and cool down afterward with stretching.
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after your runs.
  • Invest in good running shoes to prevent injuries.
  • Listen to your body and take rest days seriously.
  • Track your progress and celebrate small milestones.

Intermediate Training Plan - Detailed

This 8-week plan is designed for runners who can comfortably run 5k and aim to improve their speed, endurance, and consistency. The plan includes interval training, tempo runs, and long-distance runs to help you achieve new milestones.

Weekly Breakdown:

  • Week 1:

    Focus: Establishing a consistent running routine and introducing interval training.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 5 min warm-up jog + 3 min moderate run + 1 min walk (Repeat 6 times)
    • Day 2: 30 min easy jog
    • Day 3: Rest or light cross-training
    • Day 4: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 15 min at threshold pace + 5 min cool-down
    • Day 5: 20 min recovery jog
    • Day 6: Long run - 8 km at a steady pace
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 2:

    Focus: Increasing distance and introducing hill training.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: Hill repeats - 5 min warm-up jog + 100m hill sprint (Repeat 6 times with walk recovery)
    • Day 2: 30 min easy jog
    • Day 3: Rest or yoga
    • Day 4: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 20 min at threshold pace + 5 min cool-down
    • Day 5: Interval training - 4x 800m at 80% effort with 3 min rest between intervals
    • Day 6: Long run - 10 km at a steady pace
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 3:

    Focus: Improving speed and endurance through interval training.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: Speed intervals - 6x 400m at 90% effort with 2 min recovery jog
    • Day 2: 30 min recovery jog
    • Day 3: Rest
    • Day 4: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 25 min at threshold pace + 5 min cool-down
    • Day 5: Hill repeats - 6x 150m uphill sprints with jog recovery
    • Day 6: Long run - 12 km at a steady pace
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Week 4:

    Focus: Recovery week with reduced intensity and mileage.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: 20 min easy jog
    • Day 2: Rest
    • Day 3: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 20 min at threshold pace
    • Day 4: Rest or yoga
    • Day 5: 30 min easy jog
    • Day 6: Long run - 8 km at a steady pace
    • Day 7: Rest

  • Weeks 5-8:

    Focus: Progressively increasing intensity and achieving peak performance.
    Schedule:

    • Speed workouts on Mondays, including intervals of 400m, 800m, and 1km at 85-90% effort.
    • Tempo runs on Wednesdays with gradual increases in threshold pace duration.
    • Long runs on Saturdays, increasing distance by 1-2 km weekly, culminating in a 16 km run by week 8.
    • Active recovery or rest on other days.

Tips for Intermediate Runners:

  • Incorporate strength training twice a week to improve muscle endurance and prevent injuries.
  • Monitor your pace during tempo and interval runs to avoid overtraining.
  • Hydrate and fuel properly during long runs to sustain energy.
  • Listen to your body and take extra rest days if needed.

Advanced Training Plan - Detailed

This 12-week plan is tailored for experienced runners aiming to push their limits and achieve personal bests. The plan includes high-intensity interval training (HIIT), pace-specific runs, and advanced endurance workouts.

Weekly Breakdown:

  • Week 1-4:

    Focus: Building base fitness with high-intensity runs and strength training.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: HIIT session - 10 min warm-up + 8x 200m sprints at 90% effort with 1 min rest.
    • Day 2: 40 min steady run at 70% effort.
    • Day 3: Rest or strength training (squats, lunges, core exercises).
    • Day 4: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 25 min at threshold pace + 5 min cool-down.
    • Day 5: Fartlek run - 10 min jog + alternating 1 min fast, 2 min slow for 20 min.
    • Day 6: Long run - 14 km at a steady pace.
    • Day 7: Rest.

  • Week 5-8:

    Focus: Increasing mileage and speed endurance.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: Speed intervals - 6x 800m at 85% effort with 2 min recovery jog.
    • Day 2: Recovery run - 30 min at an easy pace.
    • Day 3: Rest or yoga.
    • Day 4: Hill training - 10 min warm-up + 8x 150m hill sprints with jog recovery.
    • Day 5: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 30 min at threshold pace.
    • Day 6: Long run - 18 km at a steady pace with negative splits (faster finish).
    • Day 7: Rest.

  • Week 9-12:

    Focus: Peaking and race preparation.
    Schedule:

    • Day 1: Pyramid intervals - 400m, 600m, 800m, 600m, 400m at 85-90% effort.
    • Day 2: 40 min recovery jog.
    • Day 3: Rest or light strength training.
    • Day 4: Tempo run - 10 min warm-up + 35 min at race pace + 5 min cool-down.
    • Day 5: Fartlek run - 20 min alternating 2 min fast, 2 min slow.
    • Day 6: Long run - 20 km at steady pace, incorporating race pace for the last 5 km.
    • Day 7: Rest.

Tips for Advanced Runners:

  • Incorporate dynamic warm-ups before each run to prevent injuries.
  • Use a heart rate monitor to ensure you're training in the optimal zones.
  • Plan your nutrition and hydration strategy for long runs and races.
  • Include foam rolling and stretching sessions to aid recovery.
